## 3. SUBMISSION FORM — EXACT FIELDS (Jotform)

**Platform:** Jotform  
**Form URL:** https://form.jotform.com/260073925589568  
**All 12 categories use the SAME form.**

### Form Fields (in order):

1. **Name** — First Name + Last Name (two fields)
2. **Date of Birth** — Date picker (must be between 15–35 years old)
3. **Email** — example@example.com format
4. **Nationality** — Dropdown of all countries (includes note: "Also open to African Diaspora but you must mention your country of origin")
5. **Address** — Street Address, Street Address Line 2, City, State/Province, Postal Code, Country (full dropdown)
6. **Nature of Submission** — Dropdown: "Group Project" or "Individual Project" *(Note: only one submission per group required)*
7. **Phone Number** — Area Code + Phone Number
8. **Award Category** — (dropdown — select from 12 categories + Presidential Award)
9. **Project Name** — Text field
10. **Project Introduction Essay** — **800 words max, no plagiarism** (text area)
11. **CAPTCHA** — "Please verify that you are human"
12. **Submit** button

### Key Submission Notes:
- **No file upload field visible on the Jotform** — the essay is entered directly in the form
- **However:** T&C #12 states: *"Group Entries must be submitted by the group leader and the project paper must indicate that it is a group project listing all the team members. Kindly respond to your submission acknowledgement email with your prototype or project paper."*
- **This means:** After submitting the Jotform, you receive an acknowledgement email. You must then **reply to that email** with your prototype or project paper attached.
- The "project paper" is a separate document sent via email, NOT uploaded in the form.

### What the Project Paper Should Include (inferred):
- Full project description beyond the 800-word essay
- Technical details, architecture, methodology
- Prototype documentation (if applicable)
- For group projects: list of ALL team members
- Evidence of functionality (screenshots, demo links, etc.)

## 6. EDUCATION ENHANCEMENT CATEGORY — DEEP ANALYSIS

### What the Category Description Tells Us:

The description uses three specific adjectives:
1. **Inclusively** — must serve diverse learners (rural, urban, disabled, different languages, income levels)
2. **Sustainably** — not a one-off hackathon project; must have long-term viability
3. **Efficiently** — measurable improvement in educational outcomes or resource utilization

It targets TWO audiences:
- **Students** — improved learning experiences
- **Educators** — improved teaching capabilities

### What Wins (Based on Competition Philosophy):

From the overall competition framing:
- **African context matters** — solutions must be tailored to African educational challenges
- **4IR alignment** — should connect to Fourth Industrial Revolution skills
- **Scalability** — the competition values solutions that can scale across the continent
- **Real-world application** — not theoretical; must demonstrate functionality
- **Inclusivity** — serving marginalized communities, women, youth

### Historical Context:
- 2024: 1,008 submissions across all categories (first edition)
- 2025: 3,257 submissions (second edition, held in Cape Town)
- The competition has grown 3.2× in one year — Education Enhancement is one of 12 categories
- Estimated ~270 submissions per category in 2025 (assuming even distribution)

### Winning Strategy for Education Enhancement:
- Address a SPECIFIC African educational challenge (not generic "AI tutor")
- Show working prototype or proof of concept
- Demonstrate inclusivity (offline capability, multilingual, accessible)
- Include measurable impact metrics
- Address both student AND educator sides
- Show sustainability model (how does it survive beyond the competition?)

## 7. PRESIDENTIAL AWARD — WHAT MAKES A PROJECT WORTHY

The Presidential Award is the **highest honor** in the competition. From the website:

> *"Awarded to groundbreaking, transformational initiatives that contribute towards inclusive and sustainable progress across Africa. Recognises visionary innovations that create a lasting impact — shaping a future of resilience, equity and prosperity for the continent."*

### Key Differentiators for Presidential Award:
1. **Groundbreaking** — not incremental improvement; paradigm-shifting
2. **Transformational** — changes the game, not just plays it
3. **Continental scale** — benefits multiple African countries, not just one
4. **Inclusive** — serves the most marginalized
5. **Sustainable** — has a viable long-term model
6. **Lasting impact** — outlives the project creator
7. **Resilience, equity, prosperity** — the three pillars

### What Presidential Award Winners Get:
- Global recognition at the **UN ITU AI for Good Global Summit** in Geneva (7–10 July 2026)
- Live Presidential Awards Ceremony (12–13 October 2026)
- Continental and international media coverage

### Precedent — MamaMate:
Ele-vate AI Africa's own project **MamaMate** (world's first offline AI health companion for mothers) won:
- Women's Innovation Competition at AI for Good Global Summit
- Global Champion for the Innovation Factory at UN/ITU

This tells us what the organizers value: **offline capability, serving last-mile communities, addressing critical needs (maternal health), working without internet/smartphone/literacy**.

## 14. STRATEGIC RECOMMENDATIONS FOR A WINNING SUBMISSION

### For the Education Enhancement Category:

1. **Address a Real African Problem**
   - Teacher shortages, language barriers, rural access, infrastructure gaps
   - Don't propose a generic "AI tutor" — be specific about the context

2. **Show Inclusivity**
   - Offline capability (huge value based on MamaMate precedent)
   - Multilingual support
   - Accessible to people with disabilities
   - Works on low-end devices

3. **Demonstrate Functionality**
   - Working prototype or proof of concept
   - Screenshots, demo video, or live demo link
   - The T&C says you must "prove your work's concept and originality"

4. **Show Measurable Impact**
   - Learning outcome improvements
   - Cost reduction metrics
   - Reach/scale numbers
   - User feedback/testimonials if available

5. **Address Sustainability**
   - Business model or funding mechanism
   - How does it survive beyond the competition?
   - Partnership potential

6. **Connect to Continental Priorities**
   - Reference Agenda 2063
   - Reference STISA 2034
   - Connect to Egypt's digital transformation (host country)
   - Show scalability across multiple African countries

7. **The 800-Word Essay Must Be:**
   - Original (no plagiarism — explicitly checked)
   - Compelling from the first sentence
   - Clear problem statement → solution → innovation → impact → sustainability
   - Written for a diverse judging panel (technical + policy + industry)

8. **For the Project Paper (emailed after submission):**
   - More detailed than the 800-word essay
   - Include technical architecture
   - Include team bios if group project
   - Include all team members' names for group entries
   - Professional formatting

### For the Presidential Award (if targeting):

9. **Think Continental, Not Local**
   - How does this benefit ALL of Africa?
   - Multi-country applicability
   - Addresses a fundamental continental challenge

10. **Show Transformational Potential**
    - Not just improving education — reimagining it
    - Creating new paradigms, not just tools
    - Lasting impact that outlives the project
